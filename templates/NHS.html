National Honour Society

ACS Cobham Chapter

The National Honor Society (NHS) is a nationwide organization for high school students in the United States and outlying territories. Selection is based on four criteria: scholarship (academic achievement), leadership, service, and character.

To get into and stay a member of the NHS, strong academic performance is required. I achieved the "Head of Schools" award, the highest award to recognise academic ability, six semesters in a row. As a result of my outstanding performance, I was able to start tutoring two younger students in math and science.

At our school's NHS chapter, we have participated in numerous community service projects, such as event volunteering, charity sales, and organisational meetings. Throughout the COVID-19 pandemic, we spent time establishing a new leadership committee and recruiting events, which inspired potential new members to continue volunteer service and leadership activities.
