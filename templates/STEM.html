4x4 in Schools Land Rover Competition

1st Place Prize Winners


Heather Coyne, Freddie Brett, Andre Horca, Daniel Wilcox, Emma Taylor, Noelle Grau

In 2020, I participated in the "4x4 In Schools Land Rover Competition." The aim of the competition is to innovate a new Land Rover car design and build a prototype, which draws heavily on Engineering, applied science, applied mathematics, CAD/CAM, Electronics, financial management and ICT. The overall process took 5 months, spanning September 2019 to January 2020, where we presented our own design against 26 other teams in the development class.

As an Eagle Scout in our American Scouting Troop, I already had leadership experience, so I took on the role of Team Leader. I managed communication and organisation, overseeing the work of the other 5 members of our team, while applying my own knowledge and expertise in areas like Arduino programming. After months of stress and hard work, our team achieved 1st place at the London and Southeast Regional Finals, and were invited to the National Finals taking place in March. Unfortunately, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the final competition was cancelled.

Alongside my role as team leader, I was the sole programmer of our ACES Innovation project in the competition, where I designed a system to analyse and map out a given surrounding environment. Using an Arduino, I was able to gather the 8-bit information sent through a potentiometer into my PCâ€™s serial port and convert it into radians around a unit circle. From there, combined with the distance sensor and an SDL/Code-Blocks program, I could successfully map out the area surrounding the sensor by rotating the potentiometer. The idea was to refine it using gyroscopes and accelerometers and attach it to our vehicle with a Raspberry Pi, but the competition was cancelled due to Covid-19 before we ordered the parts.
