2020 SATRO Research Program

In the summer of 2020 I participated in the "SATRO research programme", where I wrote and presented a research paper on Mooreâ€™s law and the architecture and design of superscalar CPUs. My research included determining which aspects of CPUs actually benefit from a decrease in transistor size, and how that impacts processing speed. This research paper taught me that computer science is much more than just software and programming, but includes much broader fields of study.

I presented my work to professors at the University of Surrey, who specialised in chip design and research. It was incredibly well received, and if it weren't for the COVID-19, I would have been offered a work placement at their research labs.
